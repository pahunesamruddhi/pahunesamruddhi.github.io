WHERE WE START

SETTING THE STAGE

What if you could simply pick up your phone, call IndiGo, and say:
Book me a flight from Delhi to Goa tomorrow morning.


No menus.
No typing.
No waiting for an agent.


That was the imagination spark behind Dottiea speculative design exercise where I explored what it would mean for IndiGo, Indias largest low-cost carrier, to have its own voice assistant.

THE PROBLEM STATEMENT

Airline bookings today are fragmented:


  • The website and app are rich in options but heavy to navigate.

  • Contact centers take forever with outdated IVRs.

  • Agents are helpful but limited in scale.


For IndiGowhose brand promise is on time, every timethe very act of booking often feels out of sync with the brand values.


So the challenge was clear:
Design a voicebot that carries IndiGos brand voice, solves customer needs, and simplifies booking into a natural conversation.

UNDERSTANDING THE BRAND

INDIGO AT A GLANCE

  • Indias leading low-cost carrier.

  • Reputation: on-time, professional, reliable.

  • Mission: connect India, support mobility, deliver affordable comfort.


IndiGo doesnt just sell seatsit sells personality. Their campaigns are fun, cheeky, and modern.


  • Use of wordplay in advertising.

  • The iconic 6E playfully positioned as sexy.

  • Contemporary tone: slang, abbreviations, emojis.

  • Humor to build memorability (lean, clean flying machine).

THE BRAND VOICE

  • Approachable: friendly, informal, easy.

  • Fun: playful, witty, a little cheeky.

  • Modern: rooted in todays language and sensibilities.


For Dottie to work, she had to embody these traitsbecause a bot is a brand ambassador.

DEFINING THE TRAVELERS

THE FREQUENT FLYER

Flies at least once a month, mostly work.

Goals: save time, zero friction, punctuality.

Pain points: delays, cumbersome booking steps.


For the Frequent Flyer

efficiency-focused interactions.

THE SAVVY SAVER

Flies every few months, leisure-driven.

Goals: cheapest fares, maximize savings, discover deals.

Pain points: hidden charges, scams, poor service from competitors.


For the Savvy Saver flexibility, cheapest-first presentation, deal highlights.

WHY A VOICEBOT

Because voice is inclusive and natural.'


  • Accessibility: for users who struggle with typing or navigating digital interfaces.

  • Speed: far quicker than navigating menus or scrolling.

  • Availability: 24/7, unlike limited human agent hours.

  • Scalability: one Dottie can handle thousands of calls simultaneously.

  • Localization: voice adapts easily across languages, accents, and literacy levels.


In short: a voicebot doesnt just simplify bookingit extends IndiGos accessibility mission.

SCOPE OF WORK

Dottie can:

  • Book new tickets (one-way, round trip).

  • Manage existing bookings (reschedule, cancel, check-in, add baggage).

  • Answer common queries (baggage allowance, check-in timings, meals, offers).

  • Escalate gracefully to a human agent when out of scope.


Dottie cannot:

  • Compare fares with other airlines.

  • Handle unusual requests outside IndiGos ecosystem.

  • Replace human empathy in complex cases (medical emergencies, complaint resolution).

PAYMENT STRATEGIES

Security matters

  • Payments are not made on-call.

  • Dottie sends a secure payment link via SMS or WhatsApp.

  • User completes payment outside the call, Dottie resumes once confirmed.


Fallbacks

  • If intent is unclear clarify once, then escalate.

  • If payment issues occur transfer to human.

  • Always offer human support as a safety net.

INTENTS AND ENTITIES

Dotties world is built around intents (user goals) and entities (details needed to fulfill them).


1. Booking a Flight

  • Entities: Origin, Destination, Departure Date, Return Date, Passenger Count, Travel Class, Passenger Name, Gender, Add-ons, Contact details.

  • Behaviour: If user says morning flight, Dottie aligns with available times. If they say cheapest, she prioritizes by fare.

2. Manage Booking

  • Entities: PNR, Email/Phone, New Date, Action Type.

  • Behaviour: Dottie verifies, executes, confirms.

3. FAQs / Queries

  • Entities: Topic (baggage, meals, loyalty, check-in).

  • Behavior: Retrieve Answer Suggest action.

4. Out of Scope

  • Anything unrelated to IndiGo.

  • Redirect politely to human agents.

HOW DOTTIE SUGGESTS FLIGHTS

Dottie doesnt just spit out one flight. Shes designed to negotiate trade-offs.

She suggests 3 key options:

  1. Cheapest (lowest fare).

  2. Fastest (shortest duration).

  3. Best Balance (good timing + reasonable fare).


  • Example response:

    I found three flights: Cheapest at 6,990 (1 stop), Fastest at 7,669 (direct), and a Balanced option at 7,313 (non-stop, afternoon). Which works best for you?

  • If user replies with a time:
    Dottie adjusts search: Got it. Looking for flights around 10 AM

  • If tied:
    Uses tie-breakers (non-stop > fewer stops > better on-time record).

ANNOTATION & ML COLLABORATION

Designing Dottie meant imagining how NLU training would work:


  • Annotation rules:

    • Consistent tagging of dates, cities, numbers.

    • Capture variations in Indian English (tomorrow morning = 711 AM).

    • Handle ASR errors (Delhi misheard as Belly).

  • Team collaboration:

    • Designers define slots/entities.

    • Annotators label transcripts.

    • ML engineers train models (intent classifier, slot-filler, ranker).

  • Feedback loop:

    • Spot ambiguous utterances.

    • Add edge cases to training.

    • Improve iteratively.

MEET DOTTIE

Dottie = IndiGo in a voice.

  • Modern Slang, emojis-in-voice, relatable phrasing.

  • Approachable Friendly, casual, always positive.

  • Fun Sprinkles humor and sound effects.

Examples:

  • Small talk: Early morning flights? You must be a morning person.

  • Empathy: I know this isnt ideal, but lets fix it together.
