SMARTCOIN

A BRIDGE BETWEEN BORROWING AND SAVING

WANT A QUICK OVERVIEW BEFORE YOU DIVE IN?

WHERE WE START

THE SETUP

SmartCoin was killing it.

  • 1.1 crore users.

  • Instant loans.

  • Minimal paperwork.

For Indias underbanked, it was the financial lifeline they needed.


But heres the twist: SmartCoin wasnt just a lending app.
It already had savings featuresgold deposits, rewards, investments. On paper, it looked balanced: borrow today, save tomorrow.


In reality? Nobody was saving. Some didnt even know saving was an option.

WHERE WE START

THE PARADOX

The very people who needed savings the most couldnt see it.

To them, saving felt impossible, almost irrelevant.

One user put it bluntly during an interview:


?
If I had money to save, why would I take a loan from you? I dont have money to save.


Brutal. Honest. Logical.
And it revealed the core challenge:

How do you convince people who come to borrow (for survival) to also save (for the future)?

THE APPROACH

RESEARCH METHOD

I approached this like a detective case. No assumptions, no ready-made personasjust clues hidden in the system, in the data, and in peoples stories.


Phase 1: Foundations

  • Read everything: history of money, lending systems, fintech ecosystems.

  • Audited the app end-to-end: onboarding, loans, repayment, savings.


Phase 2: Quantitative

  • Analyzed 1 lakh+ users.

  • Looked at demographics, credit scores, repayment behavior, page visits.


Phase 3: Qualitative

  • 30 interviews per user segment.

  • Dug into motivations, financial habits, fears.


It felt like staring at a massive block of stone. Slowly, by chipping away, human shapes started to emerge.

FOUNDATIONAL UNDERSTANDING : PROCESS

I mapped the journey end-to-end: what it felt like to be a user stepping into SmartCoin.

  • Onboarding: forms, friction, first impressions.

  • Loan: instant disbursal, the core hook.

  • Repayment: relief or dread, depending on income stability.

  • Savings (if discovered): confusing graphs, intimidating jargon, little connection to lived realities.

The flows were functional. But they werent designed to inspire trust, confidence, or curiosity.

FOUNDATIONAL UNDERSTANDING : UI AUDIT

Screens became evidence.

  • Homepage: entirely loan-focused.

  • Savings page: buried, chart-heavy, unclear purpose.

  • Help: dense FAQs that increased anxiety.


The interface didnt just reflect the paradox. It amplified it.

THE DEFINITION

UNDERSTANDING BORROWING

Borrowing wasnt a single behavior. It was a spectrum psychological, social, and economic.

  • Opportunity: borrowing to grab time-sensitive chances.

  • Consumption: indulging in goods and experiences otherwise out of reach.

  • Creating Assets: investing in something lasting education, property, business tools.

  • Income: smoothing volatile earnings, ensuring steady consumption.

  • Repayment: restructuring existing debts.

  • Comfort: buying dignity, convenience, or relief in daily life.

Borrowing was never just about money. It was about aspiration, survival, and self-worth.

THE PEOPLE

SCULPTING A MOUNTAIN OF DATA

Here was the hardest part: Who exactly were we designing for?


With 2.5 lakh+ users, the idea of the SmartCoin user was meaningless.


It felt like standing before a giant, shapeless block of stone. The data was there, vast and overwhelming.


The task wasnt just to measure it it was to sculpt it. To slowly chip away at noise until the outlines of real people began to emerge.

HOW DO YOU NARROW DOWN FROM 2.5L?

We started with numbers: a sample of 1 lakh users.

We asked: Who are they? Where do they live? Whats their credit profile? How do they move inside the app?

Three lenses brought order:


  • Demographics (age, location, employment)

  • Financial profile (credit scores, loan eligibility)

  • In-app activity (loan amounts, repayment behavior, most visited pages)


Patterns emerged. Clusters formed. The stone began to crack open.

GIVING THE SEGMENTS A VOICE

But numbers alone cant explain human logic. Data only says what. It never says why.


So we sat with users. We asked about choices, struggles, and the stories behind every loan.


This is where the paradox spoke loudest. Over and over, we heard:
If I had money to save, why would I take a loan?


Suddenly, the clusters werent just rows in a dataset. They had voices. Emotions. Frustrations.

THE PEOPLE

WHO ARE WE DESIGNING FOR?

Through this layered process, three distinct archetypes emerged the anchors for our design work:

The Eternal Borrower

Imagine someone living on the edge of their income. Rent, groceries, school fees everything consumes more than they earn. The gap is permanent. SmartCoin is their safety net. Each time a loan comes through, its oxygen.


Motivated by: Survive the month. Keep afloat.

Behaviours: Always borrows the full loan amount. Repayment is the top priority, sometimes at the cost of other needs.

Pain: Saving feels insulting. Its not just out of reach it feels like advice from another planet.

The Goal-Oriented Borrower

Now, picture someone else. For them, borrowing is purposeful, not habitual. They borrow for milestones: medical bills, school fees, family events. Theyre disciplined. They compare options. They already think about saving, but their current tools dont align with their needs.


Motivated by: Achieve specific goals. Plan for stability.

Behaviours: Borrows only what is necessary. Looks for better products. Actively seeks financial knowledge

Pain: No clear bridge between short-term loans and long-term goals.

The Profit-Driven Investor

Finally, imagine a small business owner. To them, a loan isnt debt its leverage. Money borrowed today is capital to grow tomorrow. Theyre sharp, opportunistic, and focused on ROI. For them, credit is a tool, not a burden.


Motivated by: Business expansion, profit.

Behaviours: Borrows maximum amounts. Often repays early to cut interest. Rarely saves in traditional ways extra cash goes back into the business.

Pain: Credit ceilings. Slow disbursal times. They need more, faster.

WHY THESE STEPS MATTERED

Heres the key:


From the outside, The Eternal Borrower and The Profit-Driven Investor looked identical. Both maxed out loans every time.


But motivations were worlds apart:

  • One barely surviving.

  • One building growth.


Without the qualitative work, wed have collapsed them into one and designed solutions for nobody.

USER JOURNEY

With our three archetypes defined, the next step was to walk in their shoes tracing how they actually moved through SmartCoin and beyond. We mapped each stage of their journey, from onboarding to repayment, to uncover where intentions collided with reality.

BEHAVIORAL STATEMENT

When users driven by an immediate need for cash want to secure a loan as quickly and easily as possible, they will follow the single, transactional path focused exclusively on their loan status and remain completely unaware of the app's 'buried' wealth-building features like Gold Savings and Rewards, as measured by app analytics showing low traffic on non-lending pages and qualitative interviews confirming a profound lack of awareness and the powerful mental block that saving is an unattainable luxury for borrowers

THE INSIGHT

BORROWING OVERSHADOWS SAVING

With three archetypes defined, the next step was to trace their paths through SmartCoin. What we found was sobering.


No matter who we looked at the Eternal Borrower surviving paycheck to paycheck, the Goal-Oriented Borrower chasing milestones, or the Profit-Driven Investor fueling growth every journey collapsed into the same loop:


borrow repay exit


That was it.

The app gave them money. They used it. They repaid. And they left.


The supposed second half of the story saving, rewards, wealth-building remained invisible. Not just undiscovered, but psychologically impossible.

THE THREE BALANCES

The deeper we looked, the clearer it became: this wasnt a simple issue of users dont save. It was a clash of tensions baked into the product itself.

IMMEDIATE NEED vs. LONG-TERM GOAL

  • SmartCoin delivered instant loans flawlessly.

  • But it failed to surface the path to long-term stability.

SPEED vs. UNDERSTANDING

  • The app optimized for frictionless borrowing.

  • But financial concepts remained unexplained, leaving users rushed and unconfident.

BORROWING vs. SAVING

By treating these as separate, competing actions, the app reinforced a damaging belief: saving is only possible with surplus money.

The app solved todays pain, but erased tomorrows possibility.

THE SYNTHESIS

DESIGN PRINCIPLES

We needed a new principles a way to resolve these tensions and rebuild the product around balance.

ENCOURAGING SAVING

Every feature must connect todays needs with tomorrows security. Saving and borrowing cant live in silos they must feed each other.

DISCOVERABILITY

The path to financial wellness must be visible, obvious, and natural. No buried gardens.

SIMPLIFICATION

Financial concepts must be demystified. Confidence grows only when clarity is present.

These werent abstract ideals. They became the lens through which every idea, sketch, and design decision was tested.

IDEATION

Using these principles as anchors, we sketched interventions

THE DESIGN

ENCOURAGING SAVINGS

SmartCoins redesign wasnt about adding shiny new tools. It was about taking what already existed hidden, intimidating, ignored and making it visible, simple, and human.

THE DESIGN

A SAVINGS PAGE REBUILT

The old savings page was like a forgotten garden:

  • Buried deep in navigation.

  • Overloaded with charts and scores.

  • Designed for financial experts, not everyday borrowers.

Users described it as confusing, irrelevant, even scary.

MAKING THE SCORE UNDERSTANDABLE

We rebuilt it from the ground up.

  • Old score out of 10 scrapped. It was abstract, meaningless.

  • New model: Save daily for 7 days earn rewards.

  • A clear, tangible challenge. Simple enough for anyone, motivating enough to try.


The shift was subtle but powerful: from an intimidating financial health score to a daily, achievable action.

CHILLAR: SAVE WHILE YOU BORROW

The paradoxs sharpest edge came from this user statement:


If I had money to save, why would I take a loan?


The belief was clear: saving required surplus. Borrowers had none.

Chillar flipped that idea.


  • Each repayment automatically triggered a tiny saving loose change.

  • Saving no longer demanded a conscious decision. It became a side-effect of repayment.

  • What was once the end of the journey (repayment) became the beginning of a new one.

Why it worked:


  • Borrowing and saving were no longer opposites. They were tied together.

  • The barrier dropped from I need extra to I can save without even trying.


Chillar wasnt just a feature. It was a mindset shift: saving is possible even when youre borrowing.

THE DESIGN

IMPROVING DISCOVERABILITY

If savings were to matter, they had to be impossible to miss.


  • Progressive Onboarding
    No more overwhelming forms at the start. New users were introduced step by step. Features unfolded gradually, building confidence instead of confusion.

A NEW HOMEPAGE

The homepage shifted from a single-purpose loan tracker into a financial dashboard.


  • Credit Score, Rewards, and Gold Savings surfaced upfront.

  • Subtle quick action animations nudged users toward features without distracting them.


The homepage no longer screamed loan app. It now whispered your financial toolkit.

CLOSING THE LOOP

One discovery was almost tragic: users didnt know their rewards could be redeemed. They were earning coins and letting them rot.


The fix? A popup loop closer.

  • Each time coins were earned, a message appeared: Youve earned X coins. Convert them to gold now.

  • Immediate awareness. Immediate action.


A small detail. A big shift in perception.

THE DESIGN

SIMPLIFICATION

SmartCoin excelled at speed. But in the rush to deliver instant loans, it created invisible friction confusion and intimidation.

A GOAL ORIENTED UI

On every major screen, a single, clear element highlighted the most important action.
Users always knew what to do next. Cognitive load dropped.

SIMPLIFYING HELP : A CONVERSATION NOT A LIST

The old Help section was a wall of FAQs. For anxious users with urgent money questions, it was a nightmare.


We redesigned it into a chat-like flow.

  • Step by step.

  • Conversational.

  • Calm clarity instead of information overload.


This turned a maze into a guided path. Users left reassured, not more anxious.

DESIGN IN ACTION

WHAT CHANGED

New Onboarding
A progressive flow replaced overwhelming forms. Features revealed gradually, creating trust and curiosity.


New Homepage
No longer a loan-only tracker. Now a dashboard where credit scores, rewards, and savings stood upfront.


New Ways to Save

  • Rewards became tangible currency, usable for discounts on processing fees.

  • Chillar made saving feel automatic, tied to repayment, shattering the I cant save belief.


New Simplicity
A goal-oriented UI and conversational Help replaced jargon and overload with clarity.


SmartCoin didnt just become easier. It became friendlier, more human, and more hopeful.

REFLECTION

LOOKING BACK

This project was never just about redesigning an app. It was about reshaping belief.


  • On process: From a crore of messy records, we carved clear personas. Rigour brought clarity.

  • On users: Behavior shows the what. Motivation reveals the why.

  • On design: The best design isnt more features. Its making the right choice feel like the easy one.


SmartCoin taught me:


Designs job is to build bridges.
From immediate need to long-term stability.
From borrowing to saving.
From survival to security.
